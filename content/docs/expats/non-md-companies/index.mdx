At the Expats Service Centre, we provide services to aid companies in the process of obtaining valid relevant passes for their Foreign Knowledge Workers (FKW) Apart from Malaysia Digital Status companies, we have extended our services to cater to fully operational companies utilising ICT as their core business activity. In general, companies will go through three (3) pre-application processes prior to applying for the relevant FKW passes.

### Submission

The ICT companies are required to apply, register their company details and submit relevant documentation at: [Expats](https://expats.com.my/apps/?a=register2&type=ict) .  

Prior to subscription to the Expats services, the ICT companies are required to fulfil the following eligibility criteria.

### Eligibility Criteria

- The Company must be a company incorporated in Malaysia.
- The Company's nature of business must be ICT-related and the business is required to be active, not dormant.
- The Company must have a physical office premise in Malaysia.
- The Company must have the minimum paid-up capital required for the equity category of the Company as set out in the table below.

### Equity

| EQUITY CATEGORY         | MINIMUM CAPITAL |
| :---------------------- | :-------------- |
| 100% Locally Owned      | RM250,000.00    |
| Local and Foreign Owned | RM350,000.00    |
| 100% Foreign Owned      | RM500,000.00    |

Under The Ministry of Domestic Trade, Co-Operatives And Consumerism (KPDNKK) the following business activities are not allowed or encouraged to apply for expatriate posts:

1. Computer retail business including computer accessories
2. Cybercafe

### Activities

#### **Qualifying**

- INFOTECH: Development and provision of digital information and platform technologies such as application software, hardware design and business platforms.
- GLOBAL BUSINESS SERVICES (GBS): Provision of services utilising digital technologies including Global Business Services, Centers of Excellence and software development services.
- DIGITAL CONTENT: Delivery of content utilising digital technologies including movie and games animation, business information, digital platform content and business research.

#### **Non-qualifying**

- Trading i.e. reselling of hardware and software
- Manufacturing
- Provision of telecommunication services
- Staffing services (recruitment agency)  
  Event management
- Training and consultancy
- E-news and e-publishing  
  Advertising
- Staff Augmentation, i.e.
  - Request for resources via resource request form
  - Projects are without project managers and resources are not in charge of project management
  - Involves resource pricing table, breakdown of work permit costing, air ticket fare, etc
  - Resources has no associated service level commitment other than hours available to work and output is simply the performance of a task with no commitment that the task will result in the desired outcome
- Under The Ministry of Domestic Trade, Co-Operatives And Consumerism (KPDNKK) the following business activities are not allowed or encouraged to apply for expatriate posts:
  - Computer retail business including computer accessories.
  - Cybercafe.

The ICT companies are also required to continue to fulfil all of the above eligibility criteria during the subscription to the Expats services and validity period of any immigration passes issued.

Please refer to and download this [Checklist for Non-MD Company](https://mdec.my/static/pdf/expats/ICT%20Subscription%20Checklist.pdf) for all the documentation required by MDEC and the Immigration Department of Malaysia.
